Commercial Use

Access boundary and contact

Public access to this site is informational and research-oriented. Commercial use of the derived materials requires written permission. Public SEC source data remains public.

1. Public access

Public access to this site is for informational and research purposes only. The documents and tables here describe a research-grade study; they are not a service, product, or commercial offering.

2. Commercial use requires written permission

Written permission is required for any commercial use, including but not limited to:

  • Redistribution of the documents or any underlying analysis output.
  • Bulk extraction of metrics, tables, or scripts.
  • Integration into institutional workflows (e.g., investment, lending, audit, advisory, or regulatory pipelines).
  • Client-facing use of the corporate A/G framework or any of its outputs.
  • Recurring monitoring derived from this work.
  • Custom scoring or reporting services built on or referencing the framework.
  • Advisory workflows that cite or rely on the framework.

3. Not offered as

  • A bankruptcy prediction service.
  • Investment advice.
  • A credit rating.
  • An automated decision system.
  • A replacement for credit analysis.
  • A production risk model.

This list is not exhaustive. The general rule: anything that implies a per-company decision is outside what the corporate extension claims.

4. Inquiry process

Commercial-use inquiries should include the following:

  • Name & organization — who is asking and on whose behalf.
  • Intended use — concrete description of how the materials would be used.
  • Access requested — which documents, tables, scripts, or derived outputs are needed.
  • Outputs of interest — what reports, dashboards, screens, or integrations the materials would feed.
  • Contact email — a working address where any agreement can be sent.

Public SEC source data remains public. Anyone may independently analyze the same public SEC source data. This site’s derived presentation, scoring artifacts, compiled documentation, and research outputs are not granted for commercial reuse without permission.
