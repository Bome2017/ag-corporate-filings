Sector stratification (Phase 19)

Pooled AUROC of 0.711 is broadly distributed across reliable SIC divisions (n_pos ≥ 20 strict positives in cell):

  • Wholesale + Retail Trade — AUROC 0.742, n_pos = 30. Strongest reliable cell.
  • Manufacturing (food / textiles / chem / petroleum) — AUROC 0.729, n_pos = 85. Largest contributor.
  • Manufacturing (durables / electronics) — AUROC 0.639, n_pos = 53. The weak cell.

Smaller cells (Services, Health/Education, Mining, Transport, Agriculture, Finance/RE residual) have fewer than 20 strict positives and are flagged DESCRIPTIVE_ONLY or NO_STRONG_CLAIM. Their flashy AUROCs (e.g., Finance/RE residual 0.92 on 6 positives) should not be over-interpreted. Full table: Sector Analysis.

Bottom line

  • The full-population signal survives with documented attenuation.
  • Structural distress is the strongest A/G composite screen.
  • Two simple primitives slightly beat the composite on AUROC; the composite’s value is robustness and regime separation, not maximum discrimination.
  • Canonical R / divergence is at chance for broad Ch.11 screening; first internal evidence supports it for the divergence / masking regime.
  • Corporate stands as a research-grade SEC public-company extension: not a production risk model, automated decision system, investment tool, credit rating, or replacement for credit analysis.
