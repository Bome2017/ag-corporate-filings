Divergence-Anchor Study

An internal future-state anchor study. First internal evidence that the canonical R / divergence geometry behaves as the Phase-13 doctrine predicted for its intended regime.

Summary

Within the divergence-state cohort (firm-years where A > 0 and G < 0 — the "healthy-looking earnings, weak balance-sheet ground" pattern), the divergence / masking geometry posts AUROC 0.705 for predicting future structural deterioration — beating structural distress (0.666) and joint-collapse severity (0.468 chance). This is first internal evidence for the divergence regime hypothesis. It is internal future-state, not external.

Anchor definitions (predeclared)

Divergence-state firm-year (at FY t)

A firm-year qualifies as divergence-state if, at year t, both:

  • A_score(t) > 0 (above-median operating appearance), AND
  • G_score(t) < 0 (below-median structural ground).

Future-deterioration positive (at FY t)

Positive if, at FY t+1 or t+2, EITHER:

  • G drops by ≥ 1 z-score-unit from its FY-t value, OR
  • The firm enters anchored pre-petition state.

Negative if forward observation exists (the firm has at least one row at t+1 or t+2) and neither condition holds.

Headline reads

  • The divergence / masking geometry leads on the divergence-state cohort by 4 pp over structural distress.
  • The 36-pp swing for the divergence geometry between cohorts (0.342 broad → 0.705 in divergence-state) is too large to attribute to noise.
  • This is the first piece of evidence that the canonical R geometry behaves as the Phase-13 doctrine predicted — useful for the divergence regime, not for broad Chapter 11 / Item 1.03 distress screening.
  • Joint-collapse severity is at chance on the divergence-state cohort, consistent with its role as a near-petition severity geometry rather than a transition predictor.

Caveats

  • Internal anchor. The future-deterioration label is constructed from the panel itself, not from an external bankruptcy or restatement event source.
  • Predeclared but limited. The divergence-state thresholds (A > 0, G < 0) and the deterioration threshold (G drop ≥ 1 z) are predeclared, but their sensitivity has not been swept in this study.
  • External validation pending. A future-phase study using restatements, going-concern warnings, or material-weakness disclosures would convert this into external validation.
